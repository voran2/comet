Comets

Icy wanderers of the solar system

What are Comets?

Comets are small icy bodies that orbit the Sun. When they approach the Sun, their ices begin to vaporize, releasing gas and dust, forming a glowing coma and often a visible tail.

Structure of a Comet

Most comets have a core called the nucleus, made of rock, dust, and frozen gases. As they near the Sun, a coma forms around the nucleus, and solar radiation pushes dust and gas outward to form tails.
